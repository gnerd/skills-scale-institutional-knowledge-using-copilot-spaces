# Change Management Checklist

This checklist provides a structured approach for managing organizational changes and process rollouts at OctoAcme.

---

## Purpose

Ensure smooth transitions when introducing new processes, tools, or systems by addressing stakeholder readiness, communication, training, and adoption.

## Phase 1: Change Planning

### Change Definition

- [ ] **Change scope documented**: Clearly define what is changing and what is not
- [ ] **Business case confirmed**: Understand why the change is needed and expected benefits
- [ ] **Success criteria defined**: Establish measurable outcomes for successful adoption
- [ ] **Timeline established**: Create realistic timeline with key milestones
- [ ] **Resources identified**: Confirm budget, personnel, and tools needed

### Stakeholder Analysis

- [ ] **Stakeholder inventory**: List all groups and individuals impacted by the change
- [ ] **Impact assessment**: Evaluate how each group is affected (process, tools, skills, roles)
- [ ] **Influence mapping**: Identify champions, resistors, and key influencers
- [ ] **Communication needs**: Determine information needs for each stakeholder group
- [ ] **Support network**: Identify change champions and early adopters in each group

### Risk Assessment

- [ ] **Adoption risks identified**: What could prevent successful adoption?
- [ ] **Resistance factors documented**: Why might stakeholders resist the change?
- [ ] **Mitigation strategies defined**: Plans to address each identified risk
- [ ] **Contingency plans**: Backup plans if adoption targets are not met
- [ ] **Rollback plan**: Process to revert if change is unsuccessful

---

## Phase 2: Preparation

### Communication Planning

- [ ] **Communication plan created**: Aligned with [Communications Plan Template](communications-plan-template.md)
- [ ] **Key messages developed**: Clear, consistent messaging for each stakeholder group
- [ ] **FAQ document prepared**: Anticipate and answer common questions
- [ ] **Communication timeline**: Schedule announcements aligned with change milestones
- [ ] **Feedback channels established**: Ways for stakeholders to ask questions and provide input

### Training and Support

- [ ] **Training needs assessment**: Identify skills gaps and training requirements by role
- [ ] **Training materials developed**: Guides, videos, quick reference cards as needed
- [ ] **Training schedule created**: Sessions scheduled before go-live with adequate capacity
- [ ] **Trainers identified**: Train-the-trainer approach or dedicated trainers assigned
- [ ] **Support resources planned**: Help desk, office hours, peer support networks

### Infrastructure and Tools

- [ ] **Systems configured**: New tools/systems are set up and tested
- [ ] **Access provisioned**: Users have appropriate access before go-live
- [ ] **Data migration completed**: Historical data migrated and validated if applicable
- [ ] **Integration verified**: New systems integrate with existing tools as expected
- [ ] **Backup/recovery tested**: Data protection measures are in place

---

## Phase 3: Pre-Launch Readiness

### Readiness Assessment

- [ ] **Stakeholder readiness survey**: Assess awareness, understanding, and comfort level
- [ ] **Training completion verified**: Required training has been completed
- [ ] **Support readiness confirmed**: Support resources are ready and staffed
- [ ] **Communication materials reviewed**: All materials are accurate and distributed
- [ ] **Technical readiness verified**: Systems are stable and performing as expected

## Phase 4: Launch and Transition

### Go-Live Day

- [ ] **Launch announcement sent**: Notify all stakeholders that change is live
- [ ] **Support channels active**: Help desk, chat support, office hours available
- [ ] **Monitoring in place**: Track system performance and user activity
- [ ] **Issue tracking active**: Process for logging and triaging issues
- [ ] **Communication updates ready**: Templates for status updates during transition

### First Week Activities

- [ ] **Daily check-ins**: Brief team syncs to address emerging issues
- [ ] **Issue triage**: Prioritize and address reported problems quickly
- [ ] **User support provided**: Respond to help requests promptly
- [ ] **Quick wins celebrated**: Acknowledge early successes and adopters
- [ ] **Feedback collected**: Gather initial user feedback for improvement

### Transition Period (Weeks 2-4)

- [ ] **Adoption tracking**: Monitor usage metrics and adoption rates
- [ ] **Ongoing support**: Continue providing training and support as needed
- [ ] **Issue resolution**: Address lingering problems and edge cases
- [ ] **Process refinement**: Adjust processes based on real-world feedback
- [ ] **Regular updates**: Keep stakeholders informed of progress and improvements

## Phase 6: Close and Review

### Change Closure

- [ ] **Success criteria evaluated**: Compare actual outcomes to planned success criteria
- [ ] **Lessons learned session**: Conduct retrospective on the change process
- [ ] **Documentation finalized**: Complete and archive all change documentation
- [ ] **Stakeholder acknowledgment**: Thank participants and celebrate success
- [ ] **Close communication sent**: Notify stakeholders of formal change closure

## Common Resistance Factors and Mitigation Strategies

| Resistance Factor | Mitigation Strategy |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Fear of the unknown | Provide clear, transparent communication about the change and its impacts |
| Lack of understanding | Offer comprehensive training and readily available resources |
| Comfort with status quo | Highlight pain points of current state and benefits of new state |
| Loss of control | Involve stakeholders in planning and decision-making |
| Bad timing | Acknowledge competing priorities; adjust timeline if possible |
| Lack of trust | Build credibility through consistent, honest communication |
| Insufficient support | Ensure adequate resources for training and ongoing support |
